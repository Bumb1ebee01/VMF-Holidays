# VMF Holidays — SEO & Content Playbook (Rank-Highest Edition)

The plan to rank at the top for **"travel agency in Goa"** and
**"customised / tailor-made holiday packages"**, and to win buyer-intent searches
for every destination VMF sells. Written to be executed, not admired.

> **Reality check.** No one can *guarantee* #1 — Google ranks on relevance,
> authority and trust. This playbook maximises all three: on-page relevance (done
> in code), authority (content + links), and trust (Google Business Profile +
> reviews + E-E-A-T). The fastest wins are **local** (GBP) and **bottom-of-funnel
> content**. Do those first.

---

## 0. Priority order (do it in this sequence)

1. **Google Business Profile + NAP** (Section 6) — fastest ROI for a local agency.

4. **Topical clusters per destination** (Section 2) — builds authority over 60–90 days.
5. **Reviews + links** (Sections 6–7) — the trust signals that lift everything.

## 2. Topical authority — build clusters, not one-off posts

Google rewards sites that cover a topic *thoroughly*. Build **hub-and-spoke**
clusters. Each destination/category page is a **hub**; supporting blog posts are
**spokes** that all link back to the hub (and to each other).

```
HUB: /honeymoon  ────────────────┐
  ├─ Maldives honeymoon on a budget (have ✅)
  ├─ Bali vs Thailand for honeymoon
  ├─ Maldives vs Andaman
  └─ Manali for couples
        (every spoke links up to /honeymoon and to the relevant package)

HUB: /trip-builder (customised packages)
  ├─ "How we build a custom itinerary" (process + trust)
  ├─ "Customised vs fixed packages: which is right for you?"
  └─ links from every destination post: "want this tailored? build your trip →"
```

Do the same for each destination (Goa, Kerala, Maldives, Vietnam, Bali, Dubai…).
Three good spokes per hub beats twenty random posts.

## 5. On-page rules (every post) + internal linking

**Per post:** keyword-first slug · title ≤60 chars · meta ≤155 chars · one H1 ·
1,200–1,800 words · a real **price range** · a **sample itinerary** · a **FAQ
block** (→ FAQ schema, already supported via `faqJsonLd`) · descriptive image
filenames + alt text · **3 internal links** + 1 WhatsApp CTA mid-article and 1 at
the end.

**Internal-linking rules (half the SEO win):**
1. Blog post → its **package page** (the converter).
2. Blog post → its **category hub** (`/honeymoon`, `/family`, …).
3. Blog post → 1 **related post** (keeps readers on-site).
4. Reverse it: add a "Read before you book" links block on each destination/
   category page pointing to its 2–3 supporting posts.
5. Descriptive anchors ("our Bali honeymoon package") — never "click here".

## 7. Trust, reviews & links (E-E-A-T)

- **Reviews:** WhatsApp every happy customer the GBP review link right after their
  trip. Target 5–10 in month one, then steady. Once you have genuine reviews, the
  code can emit **`aggregateRating`** schema so stars show in results (never fake
  it — Google penalises that).
- **Author/expertise:** add a short "Planned by VMF's Goa team" byline + a real
  team photo on the About page and blog posts. Real people = trust signals.
- **Backlinks (quality > quantity):** Goa wedding/honeymoon blogs, college-fest
  partnerships (you run college tours), hotel/resort "recommended agent" listings,
  local press, travel directories. A handful of relevant local links beats dozens
  of spam ones.

## 8. "Stand out" in the results (rich snippets & CTR)

- **Structured data** (done in code): Organization/TravelAgency with services,
  Product+Offer on packages, FAQ on package pages, Breadcrumbs, Service on the
  Trip Builder. Add FAQ blocks to category pages too.
- **Compelling meta descriptions** — they don't rank you but they win the *click*:
  lead with the benefit + a soft CTA ("Free quote on WhatsApp.").
- **Differentiation angle to repeat everywhere:** *transparent pricing, no booking
  fees, a dedicated human planner, 100% customised.* That's your wedge against
  MakeMyTrip/Thrillophilia — say it in titles, intros and the GBP description.
